Key insights on competitor homepage page structure: Miro, Canva, Figma, Notion, Confluence, AI trend

Key insights on page structure

Miro — longest scroll

Hero → AI features → use cases (PM/Design/Eng) → integrations → social proof → pricing → CTA. Many anchors for different ICPs — designed for high-attention enterprise visitors.

Canva — shortest path to action

CTA in the hero is the primary priority. Collaboration barely appears above the fold — it lives in scroll-down blocks. Page is built on virality and brand recognition, not persuasion.

Figma — collaboration is physically visible

The only brand where collaboration is shown in the hero visually: multiple cursors in one file. It isn't described — it's demonstrated. A strong design-by-example approach.

Notion — AI replaced collaboration

2023 vs 2025: the hero used to be built on "all-in-one team workspace" — now it's "AI workspace." Collaboration moved to the subheadline. A significant positioning shift.

Confluence — no standalone value

The hero is the weakest of the five because Confluence lives inside the Jira ecosystem. Positioning is built on "suite halo," not on the product's independent strength.

Trend: AI is displacing the collaboration narrative

All five brands added an AI layer in 2024–2025. Miro and Notion made AI the primary hero narrative. Canva and Figma integrated AI into features — not into the hero.
